Pinous (https://pinous.co.kr/, hereinafter "Pinous") establishes and discloses this Privacy Policy in accordance with Article 30 of the Personal Information Protection Act in order to protect the personal information of data subjects and to handle related grievances promptly and smoothly.

Article 1. Purpose of Processing Personal Information

Pinous processes personal information for the purposes described in this policy. Personal information will not be used for purposes other than those stated, and if the purpose of use changes, Pinous will take necessary measures such as obtaining separate consent in accordance with Article 18 of the Personal Information Protection Act.

Article 2. Processing and Retention Period

Pinous processes and retains personal information within the period required by law or the period consented to by the data subject at the time of collection.

  1. Other personal information may be retained and used for up to three years from the date of consent for collection and use. The retention basis is to transparently inform users how sensitive user and device data is handled. The related retention period for records concerning collection, processing, and use of credit information is three years.

Article 3. Destruction Procedure and Method

Pinous destroys personal information without delay when it becomes unnecessary, such as when the retention period expires or the processing purpose has been achieved.

If personal information must continue to be preserved under other laws after the retention period has expired or the processing purpose has been achieved, the information will be moved to a separate database or stored in a different location. Preserved items may include account information and transaction dates.

Pinous selects personal information for which a destruction reason has occurred and destroys it with the approval of the privacy officer. Electronic files are destroyed using technical methods that prevent recovery, and printed documents are shredded or incinerated.

Article 4. Rights and Duties of Data Subjects and Legal Representatives

Data subjects may exercise rights such as access, correction, deletion, and suspension of processing of personal information at any time. These rights may be exercised in writing, by email, fax, or other methods under applicable law, and Pinous will respond without delay.

Rights may also be exercised through a legal representative or authorized agent. In such cases, a power of attorney under the applicable public notice must be submitted. Requests for access and suspension of processing may be restricted by law, and deletion may not be requested where other laws require the personal information to be collected.

Pinous verifies whether the person making the request is the data subject or a legitimate representative.

Article 5. Measures to Ensure Safety of Personal Information

  • Regular internal audits are conducted to ensure the safe handling of personal information.
  • Access to personal information is limited to designated personnel, and relevant training is provided.
  • Important data is protected through encryption or other security functions for files and transmissions.

Article 6. Cookies

Pinous does not use cookies that store and retrieve user information.

Article 7. Behavioral Information

Pinous does not collect, use, or provide behavioral information for online customized advertising.

Article 8. Criteria for Additional Use or Provision

Pinous may additionally use or provide personal information without the data subject's consent after considering the criteria under the Personal Information Protection Act and its Enforcement Decree.

  • Whether the additional use or provision is related to the original collection purpose.
  • Whether the additional use or provision is predictable based on the collection context or processing practices.
  • Whether the additional use or provision unfairly infringes the interests of the data subject.
  • Whether necessary safety measures such as pseudonymization or encryption have been taken.

Article 11. Privacy Officer

Pinous designates a privacy officer responsible for personal information processing and handling complaints and remedies related to personal information.

Data subjects may contact the privacy officer or responsible department regarding privacy inquiries, complaints, and remedies. Pinous will respond and handle such inquiries without delay.
